Research Experience

AIED, Human-AI Interaction, and School Digital Transformation

My research focuses on Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIED), particularly human-AI interaction, AI-supported discussion, and collaborative knowledge construction in online and blended learning environments.

Empirical Research on AI-Supported Interaction and Knowledge Construction

LLM-based AI Teaching Assistant Agent for Asynchronous Discussion Forums

2025-present · Research Lead & System Developer
  • Led the full research cycle, including theoretical framing, instructional design, system development, platform deployment, empirical evaluation, and manuscript writing.
  • Designed a CoI-informed AI teaching assistant for asynchronous discussion forums, targeting cognitive presence, social presence, and teaching presence.
  • Developed and integrated an LLM-based teaching assistant system into an online course discussion platform through API-based implementation.
  • Deployed the system across five university courses involving over 300 students.
  • Conducted a 10-week quasi-experimental mixed-methods study comparing AI TA-supported and human TA-supported discussions.
  • Collected and analyzed CoI questionnaires, forum transcripts, reflection posts, and semi-structured interviews.
  • Identified a perception-behavior gap in AI-supported discussions: the AI TA enhanced students' perceived CoI presence, while its influence on deeper collaborative inquiry behaviors remained limited.
  • Produced multiple research outputs, including a master's thesis, an accepted GCCCE 2026 conference paper, and an English journal manuscript under review.

Public AI Invocation and AI-Mediated Knowledge Construction on Social Media

2026-present · Research Lead, First Author & Corresponding Author
  • Investigated how conversational AI agents are invoked and taken up in public social media discussions as part of human-AI-human knowledge construction.
  • Analyzed 869 knowledge-related AI-invocation episodes from rehydrated GrokSet conversations using interactional pattern analysis and an adapted Interaction Analysis Model.
  • Developed an episode-level coding framework to identify public AI invocation patterns and observable levels of knowledge construction.
  • Used LLM-assisted screening and coding with double human verification to support efficient and reliable data analysis.
  • Identified six interactional patterns and found that deeper knowledge construction emerged mainly through evidence testing, argumentative intervention, and post-response uptake.
  • Prepared a conference paper currently under review.

Theoretical Research on Human-AI Interaction in Education

Teaching Subjectivity and Human-AI Relationships in the Intelligent Era

2025-2026 · Lead Student Researcher & Corresponding Author
  • Led the conceptual development and manuscript drafting of a theoretical study on teaching subjectivity in the intelligent era.
  • Drew on posthumanist perspectives to rethink anthropocentric assumptions in traditional teaching theory.
  • Proposed that teaching subjectivity is shifting from human individuals to human-machine symbiotic networks, from static presupposition to dynamic becoming, and from hierarchical opposition to distributed collaboration.
  • Discussed ethical risks in AI-supported teaching, including responsibility fragmentation, erosion of teaching autonomy, and marginalization of humanistic values.
  • Proposed governance pathways such as meaningful human control, teachers' critical digital literacy, and the balance between instrumental rationality and value rationality.
